Here are some typical terms and conditions you might encounter:

Scope of Services: This section outlines the specific services or products that the Gigapay Pvt. Ltd. will provide to the client. It may include details about software development, IT consulting, technical support, or other IT-related services.


Payment Terms: This section specifies the payment terms, such as pricing, invoicing schedule, and accepted payment methods. It may also include information about late payment penalties or interest charges for overdue invoices.


Intellectual Property: This section addresses the ownership of intellectual property rights. It clarifies whether the client or the Gigapay Pvt. Ltd. will retain ownership of any software, code, or other intellectual property developed during the engagement.


Confidentiality: This section establishes the obligations of both parties to keep any confidential information exchanged during the engagement confidential. It may also include non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) to protect sensitive information.


Data Protection and Privacy: This section outlines how the Gigapay Pvt. Ltd. will handle and protect the client’s data and comply with relevant data protection and privacy laws. It may include details about data security measures, data storage, data access, and data breach notifications.


Termination and Cancellation: This section describes the conditions under which either party can terminate the agreement, such as breach of contract, non-payment, or other specified circumstances. It may also include provisions for notice periods and any associated termination fees or penalties.


Warranty and Liability: This section covers the warranties provided by the Gigapay Pvt. Ltd. for their services or products. It may specify the extent of liability in case of service disruptions, errors, or damages caused by the Gigapay Pvt. Ltd.’s products or services.


Dispute Resolution: This section outlines the procedures for resolving any disputes that may arise between the client and the Gigapay Pvt. Ltd. It may specify whether disputes will be settled through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or through the court system.


Indemnification: This section addresses the responsibilities of each party to indemnify and hold the other party harmless from any losses, damages, or liabilities arising from the engagement.


Governing Law and Jurisdiction: This section determines which laws will govern the agreement and which jurisdiction’s courts will have jurisdiction over any disputes.
